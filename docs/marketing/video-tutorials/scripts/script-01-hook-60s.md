# Script 01 — "What is Quidnug?" (60-second hook)

**Length:** 0:60
**Audience:** Cold traffic (LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube Shorts).
**Goal:** Make someone curious enough to click through.
**Recommended stack:** Real voice (yours) + ElevenLabs for any
follow-up cuts + Runway Gen-4 B-roll + Descript.

---

## Script

**[0:00–0:05] Hook — text on screen + brief B-roll**

> "Your bank has 'approved' you. Your vendor is 'verified.' Your
> contractor has '5 stars.' Do any of those actually mean
> anything to **you**?"

*Visual cue: rapid cuts of familiar UI — bank app, Yelp stars,
"verified" badges on social. End on a question mark.*

**[0:05–0:15] Problem**

> "Trust today is one-size-fits-all. A single reputation score.
> A single 'approved' flag. But your bank trusts a vendor
> differently than you do. Your company trusts a contractor
> differently than your neighbor does. Real trust is personal —
> and we've been pretending it isn't."

*Visual cue: single monolithic "TRUST SCORE: 87" dissolves into
thousands of personalized-view edges.*

**[0:15–0:35] Solution — what is Quidnug**

> "Quidnug is a decentralized protocol for **relational trust** —
> trust that's personal, transitive, and cryptographic."
>
> "Every person, organization, AI agent, and device is a
> **quid** — a cryptographic identity you control. You issue
> signed trust edges to the people, orgs, and agents you
> actually trust."
>
> "From your perspective, Quidnug computes the transitive trust
> across the graph. 'Alice trusts Carol at 0.9, Carol trusts
> Bob at 0.8, so from Alice's perspective, Bob is trusted at
> 0.72.' Every observer gets their own answer."

*Visual cue: animated trust graph filling out with signed edges,
then a "camera move" from observer A's POV to observer B's POV,
showing how the computed scores differ.*

**[0:35–0:50] Proof of substance**

> "It works today. Apache-2.0. SDKs for Python, Go, JavaScript,
> Rust, Java, .NET, and Swift. Drop-in for AI agent identity,
> election integrity, W3C Verifiable Credentials, supply-chain
> attestation, and more."

*Visual cue: language logos appear as a row; then the word
"production" appears over a terminal running the CLI.*

**[0:50–0:60] Call to action**

> "Per-observer trust. No central authority. Open source. See
> how it works at **github.com/bhmortim/quidnug**."

*Visual cue: URL holds; fade to logo.*

---

## Production notes

- **Voice**: Real you if possible. An AI voice for a 60-sec hero
  video reads as inauthentic; a real voice (even lightly edited)
  builds trust for the next 17 videos in the series.
- **B-roll** via Runway Gen-4, prompted with phrases like:
  - "abstract network of connected glowing nodes, slow camera
    move, cinematic, dark background, blue-to-violet palette"
  - "binary data flowing between two crystalline shapes"
  - "cryptographic key shape materializing, slow reveal"
- **Captions**: every word. Descript auto-generates; verify.
- **Export targets**:
  - 1920×1080 for YouTube / Twitter
  - 1080×1920 for LinkedIn / YouTube Shorts / TikTok
  - 1080×1080 for Instagram feed

## First-5-second test

Rewind and watch the first 5 seconds only. If it doesn't earn
the next 5 seconds, rewrite. Viewers on social will drop in
under 3 seconds.
